Data Preprocessing for Machine Learning



Data Cleaning
Our data has been sourced from Kaggle and The UN Refugee Agency.
Total data consists of more than 11 Million rows of tweets & 18 columns. The tweets were in 64 languages. Filtering English tweets brought the data down to 7 million and filtering by location availability, which was needed for further analysis, brought it to 2 million tweets. Regex proved to be very efficient in cleaning data by removing the mentions, hyperlinks, hashtags and everything not text etc.

For Sentiment Analysis, we faced our second challenge. Due to the sheer volume of our data, it was impossible to manually label the data for training. After going through a lot of research papers and considering a wide range of options, we finalized on VADER, a pre-trained lexicon based sentiment analysis tool specifically attuned to both polarity and intensity of sentiments expressed in social media contexts. VADER is a very potent tool with abilities to analyze emoticons, acronyms, commonly used slang, punctuations & most importantly ability to analyze entire sentences. Compound score is the most useful metric. It gives a ‘normalized, weighted composite score’ for a given sentence. compound scores for tweets are used for further analyses.

The sentiment distribution of compound scores shows almost normal distribution of sentiment scores with a slight skew towards negative polarity. It is observable both in boxplot and seaborn distplot.
